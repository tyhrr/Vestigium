A brief history of Vestigium: Building a sustainable future.

Vestigium: Responding to Community Needs

Vestigium Association (from Latin, “footprint”) is an ecological and creative organization dedicated to enhancing the quality of life in our community by fostering and promoting a healthy, sustainable future. The association was founded in 2011 by six women who recognized the need for a multifunctional space where neighbors could come together to share and develop initiatives. Vestigium was created to address pressing challenges such as increasing social isolation, the lack of communal spaces, limited activities for children and adults, and the exclusion of families and elderly residents from active community life.

A Space for Creativity and Connections

The Vrbani neighborhood, a young district of Zagreb, is home to many families but has long lacked essential services such as a market, library, health center, or community hub. Vestigium bridges these gaps by hosting a farmers market, establishing a free library, organizing poetry evenings, storytelling for children, and offering a variety of workshops and lectures. These include topics on healthy living, cooking, sewing, relaxation, and other educational and ecological subjects. Our activities are powered by the passion and dedication of volunteers and members who actively shape and support the community. Together, we aim to improve the quality of life for families, children, and individuals.

Ethics and Sustainability at the Core

Vestigium promotes ethical practices through initiatives like community-supported agriculture, which connects urban consumers with local organic farmers within a 60 km radius, providing fresh, sustainable food. We also support small ethical businesses and run workshops on topics such as no-dig gardening, urban farming, composting, social permaculture, sociocracy, and Theatre of the Oppressed. Additionally, we host swap shops, repair cafés, zero-waste events, communal cooking sessions, farm visits, and family gatherings—all with the aim of fostering resilience and minimizing our ecological footprint. Our approach is deeply local, with our community center designed by and for its users. At Vestigium, we believe in empowering people to creatively and freely shape their community, ensuring a sustainable and connected future for all.

Empowering Communities Through Voluntary Support

Vestigium thrives through the voluntary contributions of its members, enabling the association to carry out over 1,000 activities annually. These initiatives focus on educating children and youth about active participation in society, empowering women, preventing and combating various forms of addiction, supporting social services and healthcare, and fostering international development cooperation. The association also promotes social entrepreneurship, advances science and education, encourages lifelong learning, and celebrates culture, arts, and community success. Every action taken reflects our commitment to building a stronger, more inclusive society.

Recognized Achievements

Since 2017, Vestigium has received institutional support from the National Foundation for Civil Society Development and has been an active part of the global Transition Movement for Sustainable Communities. That same year, Vestigium was showcased as an inspiring "Changemaker" from Croatia at the European Network of Cultural Centers Conference in Hildesheim, Germany. In 2018, the association was honored as one of the top 30 nominees for the “Social Marie Award” out of 250 socially innovative projects across Europe, further solidifying our role as a leader in community-driven innovation and sustainable development.
